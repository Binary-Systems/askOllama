The first president of Costa Rica was Jorge Toccles Arce, who served from 1824 to 1833.
The first president of the United States was George Washington. He was inaugurated as the country's first president on April 30, 1789 and served two terms until March 4, 1797.
The first president of Russia was Boris Yeltsin, who served from 1991 to 1999.
The first president of China was Chiang Kai-shek, who served as the President of the Republic of China from 1928 to 1975.
The first president of Canada was John A. Macdonald, who served as the first Prime Minister of Canada from 1867 to 1873 and again from 1878 to 1891. At that time, Canada did not have a separate presidential position, as it was a dominion within the British Empire. It wasn't until 1950 that Canada adopted a separate presidential system, with Roland Michener serving as the first Governor General of Canada from 1952 to 1959.
The first president of Japan was Kenzo Nakamura, who served as the first Prime Minister of Japan from 1868 to 1871. Prior to this, Japan was ruled by the Tokugawa shogunate, which was a feudal regime that had been in power since 1603.
